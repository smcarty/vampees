# 🚛 Interplanetary Logistics & Data Registry

Welcome to the primary directory for the VAMPEES automated supply chain. This folder serves as the active workshop for mapping out orbital trajectories, mass-driver acceleration paths, automated freight loops, and resource distribution metrics. 

Our goal is to build an unshakeable, automated pipeline that links the nodes—from the Venusian energy engine to the Martian interstellar springboard—without relying on centralized corporate control.

---

## 🔬 Active Framework & System Proposals

To maintain a clean, organized directory, files in this sector are split by their developmental status:

### 📑 1. Core Framework (The Main Files)
These are our working blueprints and active data layouts. They hold the baseline transportation loops, synodic launch windows, and tracking metrics we are actively honing and stress-testing against hard physics.

### 📂 2. The `proposals/` Subfolder (The Dedicated Sandbox)
When a logistics concept graduates out of the main, universal `sandbox/` directory and proves it has a viable, mathematically grounded application specifically for shipping or data routing, it is moved into our local `proposals/` folder.
* **What lives here:** Specialized cycler schedules, radiation-shielding specifications, or automated cargo-handling variants undergoing deep community audit.
* **The Goal:** We stress-test these routing designs here until the orbital mechanics are seamless, the cargo mass metrics balance out, and they are ready to be integrated directly into the core logistics framework.

## 📈 Metric Standards: The Mass-Multiplication Ratio

All automated operations and proposals logged in this directory are bound by a strict efficiency metric designed to ensure deep-space self-sufficiency:

$$\text{Mass-Multiplication Ratio} \ge 1000:1$$

For every **1 ton** of high-technology components or precision equipment exported from Earth, the decentralized deep-space network must successfully extract and utilize at least **1,000 tons** of local cosmic material (from asteroids, moons, or atmospheres), ensuring the interplanetary system remains entirely additive and sustainable.
