# Asteroid Belt Node: Extraction & Autonomous Manufacturing

Welcome to the primary directory for the Asteroid Belt sector of the VAMPEES architecture. This folder serves as the active workshop for mapping out the autonomous resource extraction, refining techniques,raw-material processing and repair processes required to feed the decentralized deep-space pipeline.

---

## 🔬 Active Framework & System Proposals

To maintain a clean, organized directory, files in this sector are split by their developmental status:

### 📑 1. Core Framework (The Main Files)
These are our working blueprints. They hold the baseline concepts we are actively honing, cross-pollinating, and stress-testing against hard physics—such as automated harvesting protocols and material tracking systems.

### 📂 2. The `proposals/` Subfolder (The Dedicated Sandbox)
When an asteroid harvesting or manufacturing concept graduates out of the main, universal `sandbox/` directory and proves it has a dedicated, viable application specifically for the Belt, it is moved into our local `proposals/` folder.
* **What lives here:** High-potential metallurgy blueprints, regolith processing calculations, or mass-driver launching variants undergoing deep community audit.
* **The Goal:** We stress-test these designs here until the material science and physics are seamless, the resource metrics balance out, and they are ready to be integrated directly into the core Asteroid framework.

---

## ☄️ Local Core Objectives

Every file and proposal cultivated in this directory must align with the primary mission of the Asteroid Belt Node:
1. **In-Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU):** Developing techniques to extract water-ice, precious metals, and structural materials from C-type, S-type, and M-type asteroids.
2. **Heavy Component Cultivation:** Designing automated, low-gravity foundries capable of turning raw materials into large structural bulkheads, hulls, and raw stock to build out the interplanetary network.
3. **Decentralized Public Trust:** Ensuring all physical extraction rights and automated manufacturing blueprints remain entirely open-source and structurally immune to corporate monopoly or private land-grabs.
